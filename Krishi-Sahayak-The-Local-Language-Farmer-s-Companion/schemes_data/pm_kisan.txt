PRADHAN MANTRI KISAN SAMMAN NIDHI (PM-KISAN) SCHEME
Government of India, Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare

1. PREAMBLE AND SCOPE
The Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (hereinafter referred to as "the Scheme") is a Central Sector Scheme, fully funded by the Government of India, instituted with effect from 1st December, 2018, with the objective of supplementing the financial needs of land-holding farmer families in procuring various inputs to ensure proper crop health and appropriate yields, commensurate with the anticipated farm income, in keeping with the agricultural produce of their respective land holdings.

2. DEFINITIONS
"Land-holding Farmer Family" shall mean a family comprising of husband, wife, and minor children who collectively own cultivable land as per the land records of the concerned State/Union Territory, subject to the prevalent land ownership system, tenancy laws, and prevailing land records pertaining thereto, irrespective of the size of land holding.

3. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
3.1 The benefit of the Scheme shall be available to all land-holding farmer families, subject to the exclusion categories enumerated in Clause 4 hereinbelow, and irrespective of the size of landholding owned by the said family as recorded in the applicable land records of the State/UT concerned, as on 01.02.2019.

3.2 In the event of transfer of land ownership due to succession, inheritance, partition, or otherwise registered in accordance with prevailing State land record protocols, the new landowner shall be entitled to the benefits of the Scheme from the date of registration of land ownership in their name.

4. EXCLUSION CATEGORIES
The following categories of beneficiaries of higher economic status shall not be eligible for benefits under the Scheme:
(a) All Institutional Landholders;
(b) Farmer families wherein one or more members belong to the following categories: former and present holders of constitutional posts; former and present Ministers/State Ministers; former/present Members of LokSabha/RajyaSabha/State Legislative Assemblies/State Legislative Councils; former and present Mayors of Municipal Corporations; former and present Chairpersons of District Panchayats;
(c) All serving or retired officers and employees of Central/State Government Ministries/Offices/Departments and its field units, Central or State PSEs and Attached offices/Autonomous Institutions under Government as well as regular employees of the Local Bodies (excluding Multi Tasking Staff/Class IV/Group D employees);
(d) All superannuated/retired pensioners whose monthly pension is Rs.10,000/- or more (excluding Multi Tasking Staff/Class IV/Group D employees);
(e) All Persons who paid Income Tax in last assessment year;
(f) Professionals like Doctors, Engineers, Lawyers, Chartered Accountants, and Architects registered with Professional bodies and carrying out profession by undertaking practices.

5. FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE AND DISBURSEMENT MECHANISM
5.1 Under the Scheme, an amount of Rs.6,000/- per annum shall be transferred directly into the bank accounts of eligible beneficiary farmer families, subject to seeding of Aadhaar number, in three equal four-monthly installments of Rs.2,000/- each, through the Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) mode, in accordance with the operational guidelines circulated by the Department of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmers Welfare from time to time.

6. PROCEDURE FOR REGISTRATION
6.1 Eligible beneficiaries shall be required to furnish self-declaration of land holding particulars, Aadhaar number, bank account details, and other documentation as may be prescribed, to the concerned Village Level Revenue Official/Patwari/Karamchari, who shall be responsible for verification and digitization of the same through the prescribed PM-KISAN portal.

6.2 In the case of discrepancy, dispute, or rejection of application, the aggrieved farmer may approach the Grievance Redressal mechanism constituted at the District/State level for resolution thereof, within the timelines prescribed under the operational guidelines.

7. PERIODIC REVIEW AND AMENDMENTS
The Scheme guidelines are subject to periodic review and amendment by the Government, and the latest operational guidelines as notified by the Department of Agriculture, Cooperation and Farmers Welfare shall prevail over earlier versions in case of any conflict or ambiguity.
