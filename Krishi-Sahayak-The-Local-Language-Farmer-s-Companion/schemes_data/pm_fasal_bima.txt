PRADHAN MANTRI FASAL BIMA YOJANA (PMFBY)
Government of India, Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare
Operational Guidelines for Crop Insurance Coverage

1. OBJECTIVE AND RATIONALE
The Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (hereinafter "the Yojana") has been formulated in furtherance of the Government's commitment to provide a holistic risk mitigation framework for farmers against non-preventable natural risks, with a view to stabilizing the income of farmers, encouraging adoption of innovative and modern agricultural practices, and ensuring the flow of credit to the agricultural sector, thereby contributing to food security, crop diversification, and enhancement of growth and competitiveness of the agriculture sector.

2. COVERAGE OF FARMERS
2.1 All farmers, including sharecroppers and tenant farmers growing notified crops in notified areas, are eligible for coverage under the Yojana, subject to furnishing of insurable interest in the notified crop for the notified season.
2.2 Coverage shall be compulsory for loanee farmers availing Seasonal Agricultural Operations (SAO) loans from Financial Institutions for notified crops, and voluntary for non-loanee farmers, subject to submission of requisite documentation within the cut-off date notified by the State/UT Government for the concerned season.

3. RISKS COVERED AND EXCLUSIONS
3.1 The Yojana provides comprehensive risk coverage from pre-sowing to post-harvest losses arising out of non-preventable natural risks, namely, drought, dry spells, flood, inundation, widespread pest and disease attack, landslides, natural fire due to lightning, storm, hailstorm, and cyclone.
3.2 Losses arising out of war and nuclear risks, malicious damage, and other preventable risks shall stand excluded from the purview of indemnification under the Yojana.
3.3 Post-harvest losses are covered only for those crops which are required to be dried in cut and spread condition in the field after harvesting, for a maximum period of fourteen days from the date of harvesting, against the specific perils of cyclone, cyclonic rains, unseasonal rains, and hailstorm.

4. PREMIUM RATES PAYABLE BY FARMERS
4.1 The premium payable by farmers shall be uniform across the country, subject to the following rates:
(a) 2.0% of Sum Insured or Actuarial rate, whichever is lower, for all Kharif Food and Oilseed crops;
(b) 1.5% of Sum Insured or Actuarial rate, whichever is lower, for all Rabi Food and Oilseed crops;
(c) 5.0% of Sum Insured or Actuarial rate, whichever is lower, for annual Commercial and Horticultural crops.
4.2 The difference between the actuarial premium rate and the rate of insurance charges payable by farmers shall be shared equally by the Centre and State Governments, subject to such proportion as may be amended from time to time by competent authority.

5. ASSESSMENT OF LOSS AND CLAIM SETTLEMENT
5.1 Loss assessment shall be undertaken on an Area Approach basis through Crop Cutting Experiments (CCEs) conducted by the State Government for the Defined Area, and the indemnity payable shall be computed in proportion to the shortfall in yield, vis-à-vis the Threshold Yield of the notified crop in the notified area.
5.2 Localized calamities and post-harvest losses shall be assessed on an individual farm basis, requiring intimation of loss by the affected farmer to the concerned Insurance Company, Financial Institution, or State Government Agriculture/Revenue Department within seventy-two hours of occurrence of the event, through the prescribed mode of communication.

6. APPLICATION AND DOCUMENTATION
Farmers desirous of availing coverage are required to submit a duly filled proposal form along with land records/sowing certificate, identity proof, and bank account details to the nearest bank branch, Common Service Centre, or through the National Crop Insurance Portal, within the cut-off date notified for the relevant crop season.
